## Accessibility Kiosk Break-Outs

These are breakouts you can try that involve generating accessibility menus or dialog boxes. Once a dialog box is generated, such as with the age-old classic of Sticky Keys, there is sometimes a link that can be clicked to get to Settings (or some of these lead to an escaped browser). 

## Test Cases

 - Shift x5 (Sticky Keys)
 - Hold the right Shift key for eight seconds(Filter Keys)
 - Hold Numbers Lock for 5-8 seconds (Toggle Keys)
 - Alt+Shift+NumLock (Mouse Keys)
 - Alt+Shift+PrintScreen (High Contrast)
 - Win+/Win- (Magnifier)
 - Win+Ctrl+Enter (Narrator)
 - Win+A (Win11 Accessibility)

Accross Windows versions, some of these may vary.  

## Next Steps

Most of these provide a link you can click or a pathway to settings, although some also provide opportunities to click for more info, opening an unlocked browser.

## Ethical Note

Remember to only test on your own kiosks, or with permission such as in the scope of a bug bounty or penetration test, with good intentions of helping elevate kiosk security.
